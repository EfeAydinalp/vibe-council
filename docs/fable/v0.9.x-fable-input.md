## What the council concluded

- **Chosen theme: "Apply the proven; describe the personas; defer their behavior."** v0.9.0 **applies
  the four mechanically-proven, add-friction-only v0.8.2 keys** (additive, reversible, override-flagged,
  guard-authoritative). v0.9.1 **describes the council personas as pure documentation** (no schema, no
  validator, no behavior) + a v0.10.x design sketch + a v0.9.0 dogfood/hardening pass. v0.9.2 = release
  prep.
- **Persona *behavior* (prompt emphasis) is DEFERRED to v0.10.x.** The council found the
  **dissent-suppression / viewpoint-bias** failure mode a *new class* the existing tighten-only proof
  machinery does not cover — *"more scrutiny is not the same as more constraints."* Applying persona
  emphasis needs a formal safety definition, content-level safeguards, and adversarial tests that do not
  yet exist; that is its own council+Fable line.
- **Guide/context export stay pointer-only; `.council/profile.*` store, session/workspace store, UI/
  dashboard, and Workbench-UX stay deferred.** Zero new trust/network/store/UI surface in v0.9.x.
- The council **rejected the author's initial A→B→C lean** (apply keys → persona schema → persona
  emphasis) as compressing too much novelty and shipping undertested trust-adjacent behavior.

## Risks that remain (Fable must keep these in view)

- **Suggest → enforce creep** — the core v0.9.0 risk. A preference must only *suggest a default* or *add
  a warning*; the moment it *blocks*, auto-enables `premium`, or flips READY→NOT-READY on its own, it has
  become enforcement (a stop condition). CLI flags must always win; the guard/executor must ignore
  preferences entirely.
- **Override-precedence bugs / silent behavior change** — the exact precedence must be fixed and tested
  (table in the brief); a preference-driven default change must be **stated** in output (stderr), never
  silent, and reversible via `--no-preferences`.
- **Warning spam** — `require_usage_flag` must be at-most-once/invocation, dedup, actionable,
  never-blocking (or be cut for v0.9.0 — an open question).
- **Persona documentation implying behavior/taxonomy** — prose lenses must be clearly *documentation,
  subject to change*, not a committed schema or authority; premature preset schema risks the wrong
  abstraction before usage data exists.
- **Dissent suppression (future)** — the v0.10.x prerequisite; v0.9.1's design sketch must show it is
  feasible (additive collect-stage-only, ranking/chairman persona-blind, disallowed-pattern content
  rules, adversarial merge-gate tests) or flag it as a larger research problem.
- **Pack budget / pointer-only** — v0.9.x must keep the pack 21/21, never ingest the vault, and keep
  guide/export pointer-only.

## Questions Fable must answer (the gating decisions)

1. **v0.9.0 application shape (per key):** the exact wiring for each of `default_review_preset`,
   `require_usage_flag`, `extra_sensitive_paths`, `never_stage_extra` — where it reads the *validated*
   result (never re-parsing), what it suggests/warns, and the per-key proof/test that every effect is
   add-friction-only and reversible. Use the override-precedence table + warning-hygiene + backward-compat
   normatives in the brief.
2. **`require_usage_flag` in v0.9.0 or deferred?** Ship it with throttled, at-most-once warning UX, or
   cut it from v0.9.0 until its UX is designed. Justify.
3. **Consumption boundary:** confirm application reads `backend/preferences.py`'s **findings/validated
   result** and that the module stays findings-only (no new public "settings" API); confirm the
   deterministic trust boundary/executor still **never** read a preference.
4. **Persona documentation scope (v0.9.1):** how many lenses (the council suggested **2–3 experimental**
   vs all seven), **named personas vs a "how to think about reviewing" guide**, and the strict bounded
   format ("subject to change"). No schema, no validator.
5. **v0.10.x dissent-preservation design sketch:** the shape v0.9.1 must outline (additive collect-stage
   only; ranking/chairman persona-blind; disallowed-pattern content rules; adversarial dissent tests;
   override/observability/rollback) — enough to prove the v0.10.x prerequisite is feasible.
6. **Schema evolution (future, not v0.9.x):** when persona presets eventually arrive, are they `schema: 1`
   additive or a reviewed `schema: 2`? Record the intended path; do **not** design it now.
7. **Confirm deferrals:** `.council/profile.*` store, session/workspace store, UI/dashboard, Workbench-UX,
   mobile/LAN/voice all stay out of v0.9.x; guide/context export stay pointer-only.
