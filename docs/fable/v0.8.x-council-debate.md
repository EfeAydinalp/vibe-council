## Council review mechanism (how "the council" is used here)

vibe-council's multi-model mechanisms are `vibe review` (multiple model critiques ŌåÆ consolidated
review), `vibe full` (collect ŌåÆ peer ranking ŌåÆ chairman synthesis), and `vibe mini`. Per the task and
the project's review policy, this planning round uses **`vibe review --preset balanced`** as the
council debate/review mechanism ŌĆö a genuine **multi-model** critique consolidated into one review.
`vibe full` is the fuller debate form but is **deliberately avoided** here because of the documented
None-content ranking-parser fragility (see `CLAUDE.md` "Known issues"); `review --preset balanced` is
the safe, faithful multi-model choice for architecture/roadmap questions. Two passes are run: one over
**this options file**, and one over the resulting [`v0.8.x-phase-brief.md`](v0.8.x-phase-brief.md).

## Candidate directions

Each candidate is scored on **product value**, **architecture risk**, **security/trust risk**,
**local-first fit**, and **prerequisite readiness**.

### A. Profile application / preference-aware guidance
Turn the v0.7 advisory scaffold into *behavior*: `vibe guide` / `context export` / `project doctor`
(and maybe `review`/`diff` defaults) **read** `PREFERENCES.md` and adjust output/defaults ŌĆö
**tighten-only**.
- **For:** makes personalization real; the scaffold exists for exactly this; high perceived value.
- **Against:** this is where the **"preferences-as-commands"** risk lives. Reading text and changing
  behavior needs a mechanical tighten-only guarantee, a parser (however small), and a trust story.
  The v0.7/v0.7.1 work deliberately stopped short of it. Doing it as a *whole theme* is the riskiest
  local-first option.
- **Value: high ┬Ę Arch risk: med-high ┬Ę Security risk: med (if strictly read-only + tighten-only) ┬Ę
  Local-first: yes ┬Ę Prereqs: scaffold Ō£ō, but no store/parser yet.**

### B. Agent launcher / session workflows
Build out [`07-agent-session-launcher.md`](07-agent-session-launcher.md): `vibe init-agent`, session
bootstrap, richer `guide --write`/onboarding flows (confirm-before-overwrite).
- **For:** directly reduces per-session/per-project re-onboarding (the stated adoption pain);
  read-only-ish generators; reuses existing guide/vault machinery; low risk.
- **Against:** incremental; overlaps with already-shipped guide/export; risk of overbuilding a
  launcher (a standing project risk).
- **Value: med-high ┬Ę Arch risk: low ┬Ę Security risk: low ┬Ę Local-first: yes ┬Ę Prereqs: Ō£ō.**

### C. Project vault polish
[`08-obsidian-project-vault.md`](08-obsidian-project-vault.md) follow-ons: `vibe summarize-history`,
a capped `RELEASES.md`, tighter budget discipline, small vault UX.
- **For:** keeps the pack lean (a real budget pressure), curation quality; very low risk.
- **Against:** low marginal value on its own; better as *supporting* work than a headline theme.
- **Value: low-med ┬Ę Arch risk: low ┬Ę Security risk: low ┬Ę Local-first: yes ┬Ę Prereqs: Ō£ō.**

### D. Workbench agent-proposal UX
Improve the propose ŌåÆ approve ŌåÆ execute panel/CLI experience (clearer cards, batch review, better
proposal authoring/validation errors) **within the unchanged trust boundary**.
- **For:** the Workbench is the keystone product; better UX raises real usage; dogfoodable.
- **Against:** touches `workbench_panel`/importer ŌĆö **highest chance of accidentally weakening a
  trust invariant**; needs balanced/full review per PR; "UX" can smuggle in surface changes.
- **Value: med-high ┬Ę Arch risk: med ┬Ę Security risk: med-high (adjacency, not intent) ┬Ę Local-first:
  yes ┬Ę Prereqs: Ō£ō.**

### E. Website / commercialization docs
[`11-website-and-positioning.md`](11-website-and-positioning.md) /
[`12-open-core-commercial-path.md`](12-open-core-commercial-path.md): positioning, a docs site,
open-core messaging.
- **For:** adoption/commercial reach; pure docs.
- **Against:** gated behind the unresolved license/provenance **"Question 0"**; not an engineering
  theme; commercialization detail stays private/local; premature before adoption.
- **Value: med (external) ┬Ę Arch risk: n/a ┬Ę Security risk: low (but Question-0 gated) ┬Ę Local-first:
  n/a ┬Ę Prereqs: blocked on Question 0.**

### F. Skills / `SKILL.md` export layer
Per the v0.7 brief ┬¦10 and the reusable-instruction-bundle idea: a **docs-first** `skills/vibe-council/
SKILL.md` POC that packages "how to work in this repo / with vibe-council" as a portable, host-loadable
bundle that *references* the vault (never replaces it).
- **For:** reusability + differentiation; a natural extension of guide/export; can start docs-only and

- **Against:** unproven format; risk of divergence from the Markdown source-of-truth; must stay a

- **Value: med-high ┬Ę Arch risk: low-med ┬Ę Security risk: low (docs-first) ┬Ę Local-first: yes ┬Ę

### G. MCP / cross-agent integration polish
Extend the read-only MCP surface (better resources/health, more hosts) ŌĆö **staying read-only**.
- **For:** improves agent integration; reuses v0.4 MCP; read-only keeps risk low.
- **Against:** narrow audience today; must never add write/action MCP; incremental.
- **Value: med ┬Ę Arch risk: low-med ┬Ę Security risk: low (if read-only held) ┬Ę Local-first: yes ┬Ę
  Prereqs: Ō£ō.**

### H. (Roadmap's stated v0.8) Mobile / LAN / Voice approval
The current roadmap's v0.8: approve/reject/hold from a phone or by voice ŌĆö a narrow, authenticated,
auditable **remote** approval path.
- **For:** genuinely useful (approve away from the desk); on the published roadmap.
- **Against:** **expands the security surface beyond localhost** ŌĆö the single largest trust risk on
  the map; needs its own full security review and a real threat model; contradicts the "local-only"
  posture every recent release has advertised. Almost certainly **too risky to be the whole v0.8.x
  theme now**; if pursued, must be its own gated line, not bundled.
- **Value: high ┬Ę Arch risk: high ┬Ę Security risk: HIGH (surface expansion) ┬Ę Local-first: NO
  (breaks localhost-only) ┬Ę Prereqs: needs a dedicated security phase first.**

## Cross-cutting arguments

- **Local-first is the product's spine.** Candidates that keep everything on-machine (AŌĆōG) preserve
  the pitch; H breaks it. A v0.8.x that *headlines* surface expansion (H) would be a strategy change,
  not a phase ŌĆö it should be a separate, explicitly-scoped, full-review line (v0.9-ish), not v0.8.0.
- **"Make personalization real" is the highest-value but highest-care direction.** The scaffold was
  built to be used; leaving it purely advisory forever wastes it. But the safe way in is **read-only,
  tighten-only, no store yet** ŌĆö e.g. guidance that *reflects* a stated preference without a parser
  that turns text into actions. Whether even that much should happen in v0.8.x is a genuine open
  question for the council/Fable.
- **Sequencing:** low-risk adoption work (B, C, F) can proceed in parallel with a *cautious* first
  personalization-behavior step (a bounded slice of A), while D (Workbench UX) needs per-PR
  balanced/full review, and H is deferred.
- **Question 0** gates E; it should not block the engineering theme.

## Initial lean (to be tested by the council ŌĆö not a decision)

A **local-first "personalization becomes useful, safely" theme** for v0.8.x: a *small, read-only,
tighten-only* first step of preference-aware guidance (a bounded slice of **A**), supported by
launcher/onboarding polish (**B**) and a docs-first Skills POC (**F**), with vault polish (**C**) as
connective tissue. **Defer H (mobile/LAN/voice)** to its own gated security line; **keep E** behind
Question 0; treat **D** as opportunistic, per-PR-reviewed. The `.council/profile.*` local store and any
preference *parser/application engine* stay **deferred** unless Fable later designs a narrow, safe,
read-only approach.

This lean is deliberately debatable. The council must pressure-test: *is even read-only preference
behavior premature? Should v0.8.x instead be pure adoption (B+F+C) with personalization staying
advisory? Is the launcher overbuild risk real?*

## Open questions for the council

1. Should personalization become **behavior** at all in v0.8.x, or stay advisory another cycle?
2. If yes, must the first step be strictly **read-only + tighten-only + no store**, and how is
   tighten-only *mechanically* guaranteed (not just asserted)?
3. Should `vibe guide` / `context export` **remain pointer-only**, or may they begin *reflecting* a
   preference (still without inlining full content)?
4. Should the **`.council/profile.*` local store** remain deferred entirely, or does a narrow safe
   design belong in v0.8.x?
5. Is a **Skills / `SKILL.md`** POC better as a v0.8.x headline or a side POC?
6. Is **mobile/LAN/voice** correctly deferred out of v0.8.x (its own gated line), or is there a
   local-only subset (e.g. same-machine notifications) that is safe now?
7. Recommended **PR sequence** and per-PR **review level** (cheap/balanced/full)?

## Council review findings

The first `vibe review --preset balanced` pass over this options file (multi-model critique ŌåÆ
consolidated review; saved to gitignored `.council/reviews/`) **pushed back hard on the initial lean**
and changed the conclusion. Summary of what the council said:

- **Do not ship preference-aware behavior as the v0.8.x headline.** The document itself flags A as the
  highest-risk direction, then proposes it as the theme with unresolved design questions and *no
  mechanical safety guarantee* ŌĆö "we'll figure it out during implementation" planning, which is
  exactly how invariants erode. Rejected as a headline.
- **Recommended theme: "Solidify the Core" (B + C + D).** Headline **Agent Launcher / session
  workflows (B)** ŌĆö it solves the #1 adoption pain, reuses tested machinery, and needs architectural
  focus, not security research. Support with **Workbench UX (D)** (every PR gets **balanced** review +
  explicit invariant tests; freeze to critical-only if review capacity is tight) and **Vault polish
  (C)** (low risk, keeps the pack lean). Zero security-surface expansion.
- **Defer A (preference behavior) to v0.9.x behind a hard prerequisite:** a **formal, declarative,
  tested preference-control model** designed and documented *before* any implementation ŌĆö enumerate
  allowed preference types (verbosity, default-flags, max-budget), and for each prove/test that a
  change can only *add* friction/checks, never reduce them. A `vibe config doctor` that **validates**
  `PREFERENCES.md` against that schema (auditable before executable) is a good pre-behavior step.
- **If personalization is deemed essential for v0.8.x, the only acceptable form is "Ultra-Safe
  Personalization":** selection among a **small, fixed, hand-audited set of pre-defined profiles**
  (e.g. `default` / `strict` / `paranoid`) ŌĆö no natural-language parsing. This proves the wiring
  safely and formally defers the unsolved text-to-behavior problem. Treat as **optional/stretch**, not
  a requirement.
- **F (Skills):** only as a **pure projection** of the vault/roadmap/invariants ŌĆö ideally
  *generated*, never hand-edited, never introducing new policy; a side experiment, not a headline.
- **H (mobile/LAN/voice):** correctly deferred ŌĆö but write a **separate pre-v0.9 threat-model doc** so
  it doesn't resurface ad-hoc in PRs. A genuinely safe subset exists: **local, same-machine
  notifications** (desktop/terminal notice when a proposal is ready) ŌĆö no LAN/mobile ŌĆö captures much
  of H's value at a fraction of the risk.
- **Process asks:** a short **normative, testable spec** for "tighten-only / read-only / local-first";
  treat **Fable's architecture step as a gating review** (no trust-affecting behavior merges until
  Fable signs off on the schema + invariant tests); add a **CI localhost-only check** (no inbound
  listeners); **feature-flag** any trust-affecting behavior default-off. (The council also thought the
  three-doc / two-review process was heavier than needed ŌĆö noted; the three planning files are kept
  because this task requires the council-led artifact trail, but the efficiency point is recorded.)

### How the debate resolves (post-council)

The council's pivot is **accepted**. The initial lean (headline a bounded slice of A) is **withdrawn**
as premature. The resolved direction, carried into [`v0.8.x-phase-brief.md`](v0.8.x-phase-brief.md):

- **Chosen theme: "Solidify the core, local-first"** ŌĆö headline **B (agent launcher / session
  workflows)**, supported by **C (vault polish)** and **carefully-reviewed D (Workbench UX)**.
- **Personalization *behavior* (A) is deferred to v0.9.x**, and its v0.8.x-only precondition is a
  **docs + `config doctor`-style validation** step: a formal tighten-only preference-control model +
  an auditor that *reads and validates* `PREFERENCES.md` **without changing any command's behavior**.
  This keeps guide/context-export **pointer-only** and the `.council/profile.*` store **deferred**.
- **Optional/stretch:** the "Ultra-Safe named-profile" slice (Alt-2) ŌĆö only if the maintainer wants a
  tangible personalization taste in v0.8.x, and only as fixed hand-audited profiles.
- **Deferred:** H (its own gated security line + a threat-model doc; local-notifications noted as a
  safe subset), E (behind Question 0), and any preference parser / `.council/profile.*` store.

### Agreed / unresolved

- **Agreed:** local-first is the spine; A-as-headline is premature; H is not v0.8.x; tighten-only must
  be *mechanical and tested*, not asserted; Fable's step is a gating architecture review.
- **Unresolved (for Fable):** the exact preference-control **schema**; whether the v0.8.x personalization
  precondition is docs-only or includes a read-only `config doctor` validator; whether the Ultra-Safe
  named-profile slice is in or out; the precise launcher command surface and overbuild guardrails; the
  Workbench-UX PRs that are worth the review cost; and whether local same-machine notifications enter
  v0.8.x or wait.
